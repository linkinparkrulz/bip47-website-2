Understanding the BIP47 Privacy Ecosystem

More Than Address Reuse

BIP47 is often described as a solution to address reuse. But it's actually the foundation for a complete privacy ecosystem. One payment code unlocks multiple tools that work together to protect your financial privacy.

> Collaborative Transactions (Cahoots)

Cahoots are transactions where multiple people work together to create a more private transaction. This isn't about hiding‚Äîit's about adding noise and breaking heuristics that analysis tools depend on.

Stowaway (Payjoin)

A transaction where the sender and receiver both contribute inputs. This breaks the assumption that all inputs belong to the same person. To an observer, it looks like a regular transaction, but the ownership picture is unclear.

StonewallX2

A collaborative transaction that creates a fake multi-party appearance. Two people work together to create a transaction that looks like it involves multiple parties, confusing blockchain analysis.

Why this matters: Both types of Cahoots are enabled remotely through Paynyms and Soroban. You don't need to meet anyone in person or trust a third party. The encryption and coordination happen automatically.

> Why Privacy Matters

Bitcoin is often described as "pseudonymous"‚Äînot anonymous. Every transaction is recorded on a public ledger forever. This creates a permanent record of your financial life.

Blockchain analysis companies use heuristics to link addresses and build profiles of Bitcoin users. One common heuristic is the Common Input Ownership Heuristic (CIOH), which assumes all inputs in a transaction belong to the same entity.

Collaborative transactions break this assumption. When multiple people contribute inputs, the analysis becomes unreliable. The picture gets fuzzy. Privacy isn't about having something to hide‚Äîit's about maintaining control over your own financial information.

> Getting Started

Ready to start using BIP47 and the privacy ecosystem? Here's how to begin:

  1. Get a compatible wallet. You need a wallet that supports BIP47 payment codes and Paynyms. See recommendations below.
  2. Create your payment code. Your wallet will generate a BIP47 payment code and assign you a Paynym ID (like +username).
  3. Share your Paynym ID. Give people your +username instead of addresses. They can pay you without ever seeing your real addresses.
  4. Connect with others. Follow other Paynyms to build your network. This enables collaborative transactions.
  5. Try a Cahoots transaction. Once you have connections, you can participate in Stowaway or StonewallX2 transactions for added privacy.
